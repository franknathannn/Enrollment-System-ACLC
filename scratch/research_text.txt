Chapter 1
Introduction and Background of the Study
Introduction
Since the establishment of ACLC 
Northbay
 Campus in Tondo, it has primarily relied on manual, paper-based processes to handle enrollment tasks. These traditional practices are usually the main roots of inefficient and slow processes, prone to human errors, and provide an extra responsibility for the school administrator. In response to these long-standing and repetitious issues, studying and implementing an Enrollment System with integrated data analysis will create a significant impact in enhancing how technology can improve academic operations while maximizing the capabilities and reliability of gathering data digitally. This system also considers the challenges faced by non-technical facility officers, providing tooltips and user-friendly features to help them explore and use the program effectively.
Aside from that, there are also external factors that make these problems more noticeable. Most educational institutions in the country rarely take advantage of current technology, which is why the education system barely makes any progress when it comes to cutting-edge methods. However, some universities like De La Salle University are already using digital systems for gathering school data. If ACLC 
Northbay
 continues to rely on old methods, it may get left behind while other schools move forward with modern tools that make management more convenient.
The Enrollment System (ES) will be the foundation of this solution since it provides a digital platform capable of storing and displaying data with the help of a database, enabling administrators and future enrollees to perform their duties efficiently, complete operations on time, and reduce the burden of repetitive work. It is also capable of assigning enrolled students into specific sections with an integrated auto-sectioning feature and auto-export of class records. Through the use of this system, schools can minimize human errors, speed up administrative tasks, improve records management across the faculty, analyze and visualize data to support decision-making, and demonstrate the benefits of automation in enhancing scalability and time management. Relying on this study will showcase ACLC 
Northbay
 Campus as a model for other schools, promoting innovation while highlighting the importance of technology in upgrading educational administration.
Background of the Study
Established on October 20, 1980, ACLC 
Northbay
 has been a cornerstone of quality education under the AMA Education System. One of the oldest private schools founded by Dr. Amable R. 
Aguiluz
 V, who is widely recognized as the "Father of IT Education in the Philippines," most administrators in ACLC 
Northbay
 continue to rely on traditional methods to record student information via paperwork. While this method works, it often takes a lot of time and uses up a lot of paper and other resources, which may lead to financial burden and administrative fatigue for the staff, especially when the paperwork piles up.
With the help of modern technology, these tasks can be done much faster, easier, and more reliably. In a more specific perspective, the automation of auto-sectioning and storage will have a huge impact on an educational institution due to its capability to assign students into specific sections with a 
gender-proportional distribution 
feature and store student information, academic records, and documents — demonstrating the strength of automation and digital storage. These benefits are capable of eliminating the confusion and extra work that every school administrator currently faces.
 
In today’s timeline, private universities are already using digital systems to help manage, store and analyze enrollment records. These systems have proven to save time, lessen errors, and make academic management more organized. For ACLC 
Northbay
, adapting to this kind of system is not only a sign of progress but also a way to make work easier for teachers and students who face challenges every day.
This study aims to create an Enrollment System with Data Analysis that will be the path in upgrading ACLC 
Northbay
 into a new level. The Enrollment System (ES) will serve as a tool that automates the collection and distribution of student information management through the use of the latest technology. By creating a web-based and school-friendly system, the school will gradually benefit from reduced human errors, saved time, and improved coordination between administrators and future enrollees.
In order to validate the problems identified in this study, a survey was carried out among the students of ACLC 
Northbay
 Campus to understand how they personally experience the current manual enrollment process. Most of the respondents expressed that the current setup is physically exhausting, as having to go to school just to line up, fill out forms by hand, and submit physical copies of their requirements takes up a lot of their time and money. Several students also shared that not knowing whether their enrollment was accepted or still pending caused them unnecessary stress and anxiety. When asked about their openness to a digital solution, the majority welcomed the idea and expressed that being able to enroll online at any time would be far more convenient for them. These responses from the students themselves served as a strong confirmation that the problems identified in this study are real and are genuinely felt by the people the system is meant to serve.
Objectives
The primary objective of this study is to develop a cross-platform web-based enrollment system that eliminates the need for physical queuing and student transportation by allowing students to register and submit requirements online, while providing school administrators with automated data analysis tools to support informed decision-making.
To develop a web-based and system-based enrollment website where students can register and upload requirements online, so they don't have to line up at school.
To
 develop a secure and centralized student record management system that enables the registrar to efficiently store, retrieve, and manage enrollment data without risk of data loss.
To provide Data Analysis (Charts/Graphs) for the School Administrator that automatically counts enrollees and shows trends (like the number of males vs. 
females
 students) to help in decision-making.
To develop an Auto-Scheduling feature that captures and manages teacher assignments, room availability, and time slots to reduce manual scheduling conflicts.
To implement a 
QR code attendance system
 that utilizes the system's existing student records to automate daily tracking and improve data integrity

This section specifies the boundaries and exclusions of the research. The following aspects are 
NOT
 included in the system:

GCash
 payments). It only strictly focuses on the submission of enrollment data and requirements.

Enhanced Student Admission Procedures at Universities Using Data Mining and Machine Learning Techniques
The research done on Foreign Literature from Saudi Arabia by 
Assiri
, & 
Bashraheel
, et al. (2024). indicates that conventional university admission methods, which are largely based on generalized cumulative scores (such as GPA and standardized test results), are not successful in predicting student success in all majors, thus, causing a high number of failures and dropouts. The scenario calls for a data-driven student placement system.
 
The first point in the paper is the use of Data Mining to demonstrate that the predictive power of admission features changes drastically from one major to another and also that it is very important to use the hidden details like the grades of high school courses. It also states that Machine Learning models were implemented to determine the suitable majors for students with the K-Nearest Neighbor (KNN) algorithm reaching 100% accuracy. The research concludes that ML models are indispensable in the creation of precise, skills-based admission criteria which thus, leads to a substantial increase in student enrollment and educational outcomes.

Enrollment Appraisal Using Rule-Based Decision Support
Villaruz, R. J. G., & Diamante, R. A. (2024) studied about Enrollment Appraisal Using Rule-Based Decision Support System. The study talks about creating a web-based system that helps schools check and approve student enrollment applications. The system uses a rule-based decision support system, where student information is reviewed based on set rules for enrollment. The system was divided into two sections. 
One part was used by students to enter their information, while the other part handled the processing of the data in a secure way. The developers used the RAD method so they could make changes to the system as it was being built. The evaluation results showed that the system was easy to use and had acceptable security. Some users, however, pointed out issues with the layout and said that some instructions were not very clear. Even with these issues, the study showed that a rule-based system can help schools manage enrollment in a simpler and more organized manner.
A Practice in Enrollment Prediction with Markov Chain Models
Zhao, Y. & 
Otteson
 A. (2024) stated that the review is based on the described study, which aimed to determine the applicability of the Markov Chain models in the forecasting of the student enrollment of a university. In many schools, forecasting student enrollment is a significant predictive analysis that one needs in order to successfully run the schools. In many institutions, however, the process of forecasting student enrollment is not well explained. The authors of the study found that despite the uncertainty that had affected the educational sector, such as the COVID-19 pandemic, the difference between the allocated student population and the numbers projected with the help of the improved Markov Chain models ended up being less than 1%. Markov Chain models, thus, appear to be useful in the forecasting of student enrollments in institutions of higher learning in the future.

Evaluating Satisfaction and Intention of Online Enrollment: A Case of a University in the Philippines
According to 
Berro

 (2025) Online Enrollment system is crucial to the expansion of the school population. Especially during the pandemic,

Their study investigates students’ satisfaction and intention to use Caraga State University’s online enrollment. Additionally, their study conducted a quantitative research approach which utilizes Partial Least Squares Structural Equation Modeling (PLS-SEM) for data analysis.

Research Design
This study utilizes the developmental research design method applied in Agile methodology in creating and evaluating the proposed Cross-platform Enrollment Management System with Integrated Data Analysis for ACLC 
Northbay
 Campus. Developmental research is appropriate because the study aims to design, develop, test, and assess a system that will improve the school's current manual enrollment, data handling, and record-keeping processes.
According to Richey and Klein (2007), developmental research involves the systematic study of designing, developing, and evaluating instructional programs, processes, and products that must meet criteria of internal consistency and effectiveness. This design is well-suited for the current study because it encompasses the entire lifecycle of system creation — from identifying the problems of manual enrollment at ACLC 
Northbay
, to designing and building the digital solution, and finally evaluating its acceptability through respondent feedback.
System Development Method
This study will follow the Agile Methodology for the creation of the Cross-platform Enrollment Management System with Integrated Data Analysis for ACLC 
Northbay
 Campus. Agile is suitable because it allows continuous improvement, regular testing, and feedback from the School Registrar, administrators, teachers, students, and possible users throughout the development process. Through Agile, the researchers can make revisions and adjustments to the system whenever problems or additional requirements are identified.
Figure 1: Agile Methodology
The research process will be divided into several phases:
Planning Phase
The researchers will conduct interviews with the School Registrar and administrators of ACLC 
Northbay
 Campus to identify the current problems in manual enrollment, data redundancy, and record-keeping.
Existing procedures, paper-based processes, and stakeholder concerns will be observed and documented.
The researchers will identify the functional and non-functional requirements of the proposed system.
Design Phase
The researchers will prepare the interface design, system flowchart, database structure, and webpage layout of the system.
The design will include modules such as Online Student Registration, Admin Dashboard, Registrar Approval, Section Management, Teacher Portal, Student Portal, Data Analysis, and Notifications.
The design phase will ensure that the system is user-friendly, accessible, and cross-platform responsive.
Development Phase
The researchers will develop the system using appropriate programming languages (such as Next.js and TypeScript), web technologies, and database tools.
Features such as online registration, registrar approval, section management, real-time analytics, teacher and student portals, and automated notifications will be integrated into the system.
The researchers will continuously test the developed features to ensure proper functionality.
Testing Phase
The researchers will conduct system testing to determine if all features are functioning properly.
Testing will include usability testing, compatibility testing, functionality testing, security checks, and error checking.
Respondents and selected users will evaluate the system based on ease of use, accessibility, security, and overall performance.
Evaluation Phase
The researchers will distribute survey questionnaires to respondents to evaluate the effectiveness and acceptability of the proposed system.
The collected data will be analyzed using frequency counts and percentage formulas.
The results will determine whether the system successfully addresses the problems of manual enrollment, data redundancy, and inefficient record management.

Data Gathering Instruments
The primary data gathering instrument used in this study is a survey questionnaire. The questionnaire was designed to gather information regarding the problems encountered by students and school administrator in the current manual enrollment process at ACLC 
Northbay
 Campus, as well as their openness to adopting a digital cross-platform enrollment system. The survey used a five-point Likert scale ranging from Strongly Agree (5) to Strongly Disagree (1) to measure the level of agreement of the respondents on each statement.
Aside from the survey questionnaire, the researchers also used interviews and observations as secondary data gathering instruments. Interviews with school administrators, the registrar, and selected teachers were conducted to gather information regarding the current enrollment process, record-keeping methods, scheduling concerns, and attendance monitoring challenges. Observation was also used to study the existing manual enrollment system and identify the common problems encountered in daily operations, including long queues, paper-based form processing, and the absence of real-time enrollment status updates for students.

Includes direct messaging, file sharing, announcements, school calendar, and quarterly requirement submissions
Student Account Portal

Allows downloading of QR code card and enrollment records

Built-in live chat and announcement board accessible to admins, teachers, and students

Archive and History System
Saves yearly enrollment snapshots for future reference
Automates Grade 11 to Grade 12 promotion
Logs all admin actions with full timestamps for audit purposes

Displays pending, approved, or declined status with decline reasons and correction request option
Public Landing Page and Informational Pages
School-facing website featuring school overview, enrollment steps, live stats, and contact information

The same groups overwhelmingly selected Strongly Agree or Agree on the user-testing items for the proposed Cross-Platform Enrollment Management System — admin/registrar and teachers near unanimous,
